Interlayer Hopping of High-Tc Oxides

The interlayer hopping integral of high-Tc superconductors is highly anisotropic. This is a peculiar but important property of high-Tc materials. A simple picture for the underlining physics leading to this anisotropy is given in the following references:

  1. T. Xiang and J. M. Wheatley, "c-axis superfluid response of copper-oxide superconductors", Physical Review Letters 77, 4632 (1996).
  2. T. Xiang, C. Panagopoulos, and J. R. Cooper, "Low temperature superfluid response of high temperature superconductors", International Journal of Modern Physics B 12, 1007 (1998).

 

Some related works can be found from the following references

  1. C. Panagopoulos, J. R. Cooper, T. Xiang, G. B. Peacock, I. Gameson and P. P. Edwards, Probing the order parameter and the c-axis coupling of high-Tc cuprates by penetration depth measurements, Physical Review Letters 79, 2320 (1997).

  2. C. Panagopoulos, J. R. Cooper, T. Xiang, Y. S. Wang, and C. W. Chu, c-axis superfluid response and pseudogap in high-Tc superconductors, Physical Review B 61, R3808 (2000) (Rapid Communications).

  3.   T. Xiang and W. N. Hardy, Universal c-axis conductivity of high-Tc oxides in the superconducting state, Physical Review B 63, 024506 (2001). (cond-mat/0001443)
